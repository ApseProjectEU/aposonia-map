§ 0

Performance notes

  1. I.

    The score is to be read in sequence, from Movement 1 to Movement 5. No station is autonomous. The gulf is a single body.

  2. II.

    Each movement carries its own tempo, dynamic envelope, and dominant frequency band. These are descriptions of the recording, not instructions for an interpreter.

  3. III.

    Performance medium: a human nervous system. The listener is the instrument. The score does not ask to be played; it asks to be undergone.

  4. IV.

    Where the annotation ▲ appears, the recording crosses the psychoacoustic threshold associated with involuntary sympathetic activation (cortisol rise, muscular tension, accelerated heart rate).

  5. V.

    Headphones are not recommended. Headphones are required.

§ 1

The field

A notation of the gulf, not a map. Time expands horizontally. Frequency expands vertically. Pressure is read as weight of mark.

§ 2

The site

Five listening stations along the Thermaikos waterfront. The score is played by walking. Each marker is a movement; each marker carries a QR transfer to the station page.

I

White Tower Embankment

for hydrophone, 3 m depth

Duration08′ 14″
TempoAndante marino
Dynamicmp < f
Band60 – 280 Hz

The promenade is silent. The hydrophone disagrees. At three metres below the embankment a continuous low-frequency hum rises from below the horizon of human attention, a drone without origin, without author, without end.

Coord.40.6264° N — 22.9486° E
Recorded14:20 local, 12 March MMXXVI
Class.Anthropophony, continuous
Threshold▲ at 03′ 42″

A sympathetic activation is measurable at the three-minute mark. Listeners report shoulder tension without knowing why.

II

Port Pier № 1

for hydrophone, hull contact

Duration11′ 02″
TempoPesante
Dynamicf < ff < fff
Band20 – 180 Hz

Propellers. Hull resonance. The harmonics of commerce, written in low frequencies that travel through water for kilometres without losing force. No break. No silence. No release.

Coord.40.6358° N — 22.9361° E
Recorded06:50 local, 14 March MMXXVI
Class.Anthropophony, dominant
Threshold▲ immediate

For the marine animal, there is no elsewhere. Retreat is a human concept. The fish remains inside the hum, organ by organ.

III

Nea Paralia, beneath the umbrellas

for hydrophone and open water

Duration07′ 48″
TempoRubato
Dynamicmp ↔ f
Band80 – 3200 Hz

Between the city and the sea a negotiation: wave motion, shore currents, the distant vibration of construction, two outboards. A counterpoint without resolution.

Coord.40.6201° N — 22.9531° E
Recorded19:05 local, 18 March MMXXVI
Class.Mixed, transitional
Thresholdintermittent

The ear begins to learn the difference between wind and motor, between swell and hull. This is the movement that teaches listening.

IV

Kalamaria coast

for hydrophone, sand–rock floor

Duration09′ 28″
TempoRespirato
Dynamicp < mp
Band120 – 6000 Hz

The anthropogenic pressure recedes. In its absence the biological begins to speak: crustacean clicks, fish vocalisations, the snap of shrimp, the unhurried geometry of the undisturbed.

Coord.40.5835° N — 22.9543° E
Recorded05:30 local, 22 March MMXXVI
Class.Biophony, emergent
Thresholdnone

A parasympathetic response typically appears here. The body releases what it did not know it was holding.

V

Axios Delta — the reference

for hydrophone, wetland water

Duration12′ 44″
TempoSospeso
Dynamicppp — p
Band200 – 9000 Hz

The gulf, away from the gulf. The Axios Delta — Natura 2000, Ramsar — gives a reading of what the whole body of water might have sounded like before the hum. It is neither nostalgic nor utopian. It is simply, briefly, audible.

Coord.40.4860° N — 22.8430° E
Recorded04:10 local, 29 March MMXXVI
Class.Biophony, reference
Thresholdabsent

The composition ends not with resolution but with reference. What the water still holds, for whoever still knows how to hear it.
